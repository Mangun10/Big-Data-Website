What are Dark Patterns?

Dark patterns are deceptive design techniques used to manipulate users into taking actions they might not otherwise take. These actions often benefit the business at the expense of the user.

Common types of dark patterns include:

  • Bait and Switch: A user thinks they are doing one thing, but something else happens.
  • Confirmshaming: Guilt-tripping the user into opting in.
  • Disguised Ads: Ads that are disguised as other kinds of content or navigation.
  • Forced Continuity: Making it hard to cancel a subscription.
  • Hidden Costs: Adding extra charges at the last step of the checkout process.

Examples of Dark Patterns

Bait and Switch

A user thinks they are downloading a free application, but they are actually signing up for a paid subscription.

Confirmshaming

A user is guilt-tripped into signing up for a newsletter because the decline button says "No, I don't want to stay informed."

Disguised Ads

Ads that are designed to look like legitimate content, tricking users into clicking on them.

Forced Continuity

Users are required to enter their credit card information for a free trial, making it difficult to cancel before being charged.

Hidden Costs

Extra charges are revealed only at the final step of the checkout process, catching users off guard.
