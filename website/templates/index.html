Common Dark Patterns

Learn about the various types of dark patterns commonly found on websites

Bait and Switch

Users are led to expect one outcome but something different happens instead.

Example:

A "Download" button that opens ads instead of downloading the promised file.

Confirmshaming

Making users feel bad for not selecting a particular option.

Example:

"No thanks, I don't want to save money" as an opt-out option.

Disguised Ads

Advertisements designed to look like normal content or navigation.

Example:

Download buttons that are actually ads placed near the real download link.

Forced Continuity

Charging users for services after a free trial without warning.

Example:

Services that require credit card info for free trials and automatically charge when the trial ends.

Hidden Costs

Additional charges that are only revealed at the end of a checkout process.

Example:

Extra fees and taxes added at the last step of checkout after investing time in the process.

Sneak into Basket

Adding items to a user's cart without their clear consent.

Example:

Pre-selected add-ons or insurance that users must actively deselect.
